1. Start Here – The Basics

Set a clear goal

Choose one main focus for the next 8–12 weeks: build muscle, get stronger, lose fat, or move/feel better.

  • Write it somewhere you see daily.
  • Make it realistic for your schedule.
  • Focus on habits, not perfection.

What a week can look like

A balanced week usually includes strength, some cardio, and at least one full rest day.

  • 2–4 days of strength training.
  • Light cardio or walking most days.
  • Short stretching sessions after workouts.

How to track progress

Progress isn’t only about the scale. Look for other signs too.

  • Stronger: more weight or reps.
  • Endurance: less tired, better breathing.
  • Everyday: better sleep, clothes fit, mood.

4. How to Eat for Your Goal

Build a simple plate

You don’t have to count calories. Start with this rule of thumb for most meals:

  • 1–2 palm-sized servings of protein.
  • 1–2 cupped handfuls of carbs.
  • 1–2 thumbs of healthy fats.
  • Fill the rest of the plate with vegetables.

Adjust for muscle gain vs. fat loss

  • Muscle gain: slightly bigger portions, especially carbs.
  • Fat loss: slightly smaller portions, focus on protein + veggies.
  • Drink mostly water or low-cal drinks.
  • Sleep and stress control matter as much as the food.

5. Stretching & Recovery

Before your workout – warm up

  • 3–5 minutes of light cardio (walk, bike, easy jog).
  • Move joints through their range: arm circles, leg swings.
  • Do 1–2 light sets of your first exercise as practice.

After your workout – cool down

  • Hold simple stretches for 20–30 seconds (no bouncing).
  • Focus on areas you trained (e.g., legs after squats).
  • Breathe slowly to let your heart rate come down.

Common Questions

How many days per week is enough?

You can make progress with 2 focused strength sessions per week. More days give more practice, but only if you can recover and stay consistent.

When will I see results?

Strength and energy can improve in a few weeks. Visible changes usually take 8–12+ weeks. Be patient and track small wins.
